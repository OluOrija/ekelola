Anxiety is a normal human emotion, but when it becomes excessive or chronic, it can interfere with daily life. Psychologists define anxiety as a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about an imminent event or something with an uncertain outcome.

**Causes of Anxiety:**
- Genetics and family history
- Brain chemistry and structure
- Life experiences and trauma
- Chronic stress

**Symptoms:**
- Restlessness or feeling on edge
- Rapid heartbeat
- Difficulty concentrating
- Sleep disturbances

**Coping Strategies:**
- Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
- Mindfulness and meditation
- Regular exercise
- Social support
- Professional help when needed

Understanding anxiety is the first step toward managing it. With the right tools and support, individuals can lead fulfilling lives despite anxiety challenges.
